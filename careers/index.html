Working At NRB

Part of our mission is to “cultivate and empower every individual at all levels of our organization.” We take this responsibility seriously, especially when it comes to our trainees. At NRB, you will find a dynamic workplace environment where your coworkers are genuinely interested in helping you grow.

01

EMPOWERMENT

We encourage all of our people to have a critical eye, to take initiative, and to take action to improve our company. This empowerment is characteristic of our trainee programs. We value new trainees for the fresh perspectives they bring to NRB, and consider our ability to adapt and act on disruptive views as one of our greatest strengths. As a trainee, you will be called on to use your unique views to better our organization.

02

SHAPE YOUR CAREER

As new graduates, our trainees come to NRB at a most exciting juncture at the start of their professional careers. Here you will find the freedom and support to shape your career. Our trainee programs consist of several short-term placements, meant to expose you to different roles within the company. Whatever our trainees choose to do in the future, we encourage them to take an active role in choosing the direction of their professional lives.

03

OPEN DOOR

Our offices operate on a strict open-door policy. At NRB we believe our company should operate as one team, and that in order to work together none of our employees should be inaccessible. You will frequently see new employees chatting with our most senior executives about new ideas, difficult questions, or last week’s cricket match. As a trainee, you will be encouraged to interact with every department of NRB, creating lasting relationships with people at every level of the company.

Trainee Programs

Trainee programs last 12 months. The first 6 months consist of two 3-month placements in different departments or locations. After the placements, we work with trainees to choose a longer-term placement in a specific department, where they work for the second 6 months.

After completing the 12-month program, trainees are eligible for offers of permanent employment in the department of their choice.

The Application Process

Interns are selected in a two-round application process. Internships are project-based, with each intern assigned a project lasting 4 - 6 weeks between the dats of April 1st and July 1st. All internships are paid, with interns entitled to basic stipend, expenses, and discretionary performance bonus. Management interns are eligible for offers of prolonging their internship throughout the following academic year at reduced hours.

Submit required documents (mark sheets and CV) by March 1st.

Shortlisted candidates are invited to interview with mentors by March 15th. Decisions released shortly thereafter.
