A History of Innovation

Cageless Bearing

Conventional wisdom once dictated that roller bearings consisted of three elements: the shell, the rollers, and the cage.

The cage presented an issue when a major truck manufacturer approached NRB with a problem. Their truck bearings were suffering repreated failure in the backside and differential. The bearings had brass cages, which were breaking again and again under large amounts of load. They aked NRB to develop a bearing for them that could withstand the load.

Faced with this problem, our engineers revoltionized the design of roller bearings by simply taking out the cage. Instead, we developed a cageless bearing, composed of only rollers and a shell. This groundbreaking design was able to circumvent the backside and differential issues, completely eliminating the occurence of bearing failure in our customers' trucks.

Today, cageless bearings are commonly produced, not only by NRB but by our competitors.
